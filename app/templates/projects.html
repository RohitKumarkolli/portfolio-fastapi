Design & Development of NT Air Blasting System

Duration: 12 months

Objective: Clearing of chute jams in RMHP, SP, TPP, and Coke Oven areas.

Responsibilities: Designed solutions for chutes transferring materials between conveyors to prevent jamming caused by material moisture. Analyzed chute behavior under varying moisture content conditions and implemented effective designs to ensure smooth operations.

Tools: SolidWorks, ANSYS, PDM, DFM, FEA, GD&T

Validation of NASA Technical Paper 1316

Duration: 3 months

Objective: Wind-tunnel investigation at supersonic speeds of a canard-controlled missile with fixed and free-rolling tail fins.

Responsibilities: Recreated NASA TP 1316 experimental setup in SolidWorks and performed CFD simulations using ANSYS Fluent. Conducted meshing, boundary condition setup, and mesh independence studies. Compared results with NASA dataset using MATLAB and Excel, ensuring reliability and accuracy.

Tools: SolidWorks, ANSYS Fluent, ANSYS Meshing, MATLAB, NASA TP 1316 Dataset

Research Project (AWD Fuel Efficiency)

Duration: 2 months

Objective: Improve the fuel efficiency in All-Wheel Drive (AWD) systems.

Responsibilities: Analyzed the role of propeller shafts in AWD efficiency, focusing on losses from inertia, misalignment, and torsional vibration. Simulated shaft configurations and materials to minimize losses and proposed optimization strategies for shaft design and torque delivery.

Tools: Vehicle Dynamics Analysis, Powertrain Simulation, Efficiency Mapping, MATLAB/Excel, AWD/FWD/RWD Fundamentals, Energy Loss Estimation & Optimization
